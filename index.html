Provincial Government Digital Attention and Corporate Financial Constraints in Vietnam

The Role of Digitalization and ESG

Overview: This independent and original research explores the influence of provincial government digital attention (PGDA) on increasing corporate financial constraints, along with the mediating role of digitalization and moderating role of ESG[cite: 165, 176].

Methodology & Tools Used:

  • Python: Utilized for web scraping and text analysis. Scanned official documents from the Departments of Science and Technology across 47 provinces to count digital transformation-related keywords for the PGDA variable[cite: 627, 642, 622]. Similarly, scanned annual reports of listed companies for the DIG variable.
  • MS Excel: Used to aggregate, clean, and process the descriptive statistics for 400 Vietnamese listed companies[cite: 166].
  • Stata: Applied advanced econometric models, including the Generalized Method of Moments (System-GMM) and Two-Stage Least Squares (2SLS) regression, to analyze the relationships and ensure the reliability of the results[cite: 168, 263, 686].

Key Findings:

  • Discovered that provincial government digital attention substantially intensifies corporate financial constraints[cite: 170].
  • Clarified that digital transformation serves as a mediating channel, where government policies encourage enterprise digitalization, which in turn increases financial pressure due to high implementation costs[cite: 172, 918, 919].
  • Demonstrated that firms prioritizing Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) practices possess a greater capacity to mitigate financial stress and navigate these constraints[cite: 172, 131].
